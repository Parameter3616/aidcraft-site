See It In Action

Real questions the AI advisor can answer using your specific data.

Why is $60,000 the optimal salary for my situation?
Your optimal salary of $60,000 minimizes your total tax burden by balancing two competing forces. At this salary, you save $18,013 per year in FICA taxes compared to operating as an LLC, because your $140,000 distribution avoids the 15.3% self-employment tax. Meanwhile, your QBI deduction of $27,082 is preserved because you're well below the MFJ phase-out threshold of $383,900. Setting salary lower would increase audit risk, while setting it higher would reduce your FICA savings without meaningfully improving your QBI position. Always verify with your CPA.
Walk me through why my STR property passed Gate 3
Your property passed the Material Participation test through Test 2 — the "100-hour rule." You and your spouse logged 200 combined hours, which clears the 100-hour minimum. Critically, your 200 hours also exceeds your property manager's 120 hours, satisfying the requirement that no other individual participated more than you. You didn't need to hit the 500-hour threshold (Test 1) because Test 2 alone is sufficient. I'd recommend documenting every hour in the Audit Defense Time Log — the IRS burden of proof is on you, and a contemporaneous log is the gold standard for defense.
